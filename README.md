# IF-LIO
## IF-LIO: Intensity-Enhanced Front-End Matching for Robust LiDAR–Inertial Odometry under Geometric Degeneracy

### Abstract
In environments with insufficient geometric constraints, LiDAR-SLAM systems often suffer from degeneracy due to weak state observability. Intensity information, which reflects surface reflectivity, provides complementary cues beyond geometric structure and is commonly exploited through intensity feature extraction to enhance observability. However, existing methods typically rely on range images generated by regular spinning LiDAR scans, which limits their applicability to non-repetitive sensors such as solid-state LiDARs. Moreover, they often incorporate intensity features indiscriminately, without accounting for the direction and severity of geometric degeneracy. To address these limitations, this paper proposes an intensity-assisted LiDAR-SLAM framework designed for robust operation in degenerate scenarios. The framework includes a generalized intensity feature extraction method applicable to diverse LiDAR types, as well as a degeneration-aware fusion strategy that selectively integrates intensity constraints according to the direction and magnitude of degeneracy. Experiments on multiple public datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in localization accuracy and robustness across a variety of degenerate environments.
